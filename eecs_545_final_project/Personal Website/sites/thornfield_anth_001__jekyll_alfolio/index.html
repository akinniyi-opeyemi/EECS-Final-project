Department of Anthropology · Thornfield College
he/him

Malcolm Reyes-Whittaker is a cultural anthropologist whose work examines how post-industrial communities in Appalachia and northern New England reconstruct identity and livelihood after the collapse of extractive and manufacturing economies. His first book, Hollow Seams (2017), received the Margaret Mead Award. His current project, based on ten years of fieldwork in a former paper-mill town in Vermont, examines how oral history practices become sites of political contestation. Before joining Thornfield in 2012, he taught at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and held a visiting appointment at the École des hautes études en sciences sociales in Paris.
